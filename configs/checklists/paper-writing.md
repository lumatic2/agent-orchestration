## Quality Checklist — Paper Writing

**Structure**
- [ ] abstract states the question, method, and main finding without hype
- [ ] introduction frames the problem and research questions clearly
- [ ] related work distinguishes prior approaches rather than listing papers
- [ ] methodology states evidence sources, assumptions, and limitations
- [ ] findings separate observation from interpretation
- [ ] discussion covers tradeoffs, limitations, and implications
- [ ] conclusion does not overclaim beyond the evidence

**Evidence**
- [ ] each major claim has a traceable citation
- [ ] unsupported numbers or rankings are removed
- [ ] contradictory sources are surfaced rather than hidden
- [ ] confidence and uncertainty are proportionate to source quality

**References**
- [ ] references include title and URL or DOI where possible
- [ ] broken or missing links are flagged for follow-up
- [ ] citation descriptions match the source content

**Editorial**
- [ ] section transitions are coherent
- [ ] repetitive claims are compressed
- [ ] limitations are explicit
- [ ] next-step recommendations are actionable
